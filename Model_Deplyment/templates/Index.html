Disease Type

Our website is designed to assist in the diagnosis of erythemato-squamous diseases, a group that includes psoriasis, seborrheic dermatitis, lichen planus, pityriasis rosea, chronic dermatitis, and pityriasis rubra pilaris. These diseases share common clinical features like erythema and scaling, making differential diagnosis challenging. While a biopsy is often necessary, these conditions also exhibit overlapping histopathological characteristics. Our tool helps predict the type of erythemato-squamous disease, offering a valuable resource for healthcare professionals in managing these complex cases.

psoriasis

A chronic autoimmune condition that causes rapid skin cell turnover, leading to thick, red, scaly patches on the skin. It can occur anywhere on the body but is most common on the scalp, elbows, and knees.

seborrheic dermatitis

A common skin condition that causes red, scaly patches and dandruff, typically affecting oily areas like the scalp, face, and chest. It can be associated with itching and is often chronic.

lichen planus

An inflammatory condition that affects the skin and mucous membranes, resulting in purplish, itchy, flat-topped bumps. It can affect various parts of the body, including the wrists, ankles, and oral cavity.

pityriasis rosea

A temporary skin condition that begins with a single large spot (herald patch) on the chest, abdomen, or back, followed by smaller lesions. The rash usually resolves on its own within several weeks.

chronic dermatitis

A long-term inflammation of the skin characterized by redness, swelling, itching, and dryness. It can be triggered by allergens, irritants, or other underlying conditions.

pityriasis rubra pilaris

A rare skin disorder that causes constant inflammation and scaling of the skin. It typically starts with a reddish-orange patch and can spread, leading to thickened skin, painful cracks, and scaling.
